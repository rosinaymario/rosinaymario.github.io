Our story

My friend Luz was going to study at the library in her faculty. None of my friends had woken up early and the idea of spending another long morning doing "resis" problems wasn't very encouraging. The company and a library full of women (despite their scatological books), was a bit more motivating.

When I arrived at the medical building, Luz greeted me with a smile full of irony.

—Ready for an exciting morning?

We were reaching the end of the exams period, but the heap of weeks that we'd spent locked studying didn't seem to harm the aspirant doctor's sense of humor.

Suddenly, a blonde girl looking like she was in a rush, passed running near us carrying a huge books pile crowned by a lot of papers that were falling while she walked.

—Ros, you're losing your exams on the way —the blondie looked at the higlighted papers her friend was showing her with a confused face

—But if I have them right h... —she pointed at her books (without papers over them), looked again at the papers that Luz held and then at the trail of paper she had left on the way.

The three of us began picking them up while she laughed out loud and excused herself saying she hadn't sleep enough 'cos she had her inmuno exam.

—Rosina, this is my friend Mario —We both nodded in agreement and smiled. There was no space for two kisses with so many books on her arms.

Rosina was on Exam Mode. It's that state where you don't care about anything and your eyes shine with a light touch of madness. You wear whatever your hand touches first, even though that means odd socks. You don't comb your hair, which for a short haired guy is not a problem, but in the case of the blondie resulted... funny.

—So you have an exam today?

—Just in 20 minutes –she nodded while resting the books on a bench and organizing the papers.

—Well, good luck then!

—¡Nah, I don't need it!

—So... you're well prepared, then?

—Hmm... not so much —she shrugged her shoulders.

Questioning look on my side.

—I have the pimple of the great occasions!

—Ahaaa...

—It's the opposite of Murphy's law.

More questioning looks.

—You know Murphy's law, it says that "anything that can go wrong, will go wrong", right? —I nodded—. So a practical application of Murphy's law, if you have a great occasion, a GREAT day —she said making a fuss with her arms—, don't know... like a wedding, or a birthday where you'll appear in a lot of photos, the most probable thing is that an enormous pimple will appear in the middle of your forehead. Well, if we turn that assumption upside down, every time that you have an enormous pimple, it means that day is waiting for you with a great occasion —she nodded triumphant with a smile streched from ear to ear.

—So today —she went on pointing the shining and pinkish pimple on the bridge of her nose—, it's gonna be a great day and the exam can only end well!

—An overwhelming logic —I nodded while I bit my tongue trying not to explain why all her reasoning was wrong. Luz held back a laughter.

She bowed, accepting with elegance the success of her proposal and began to pick her pile of books again.

—The theory of the pimple of the great occasions —she insisted while she tapped her temple—. It never fails!

And with a huge smile, she turned to go upstairs.

—I'll see you when I'm done —she said waving her hand goodbye while several papers slipped between her arms and rolled down the stairs.
